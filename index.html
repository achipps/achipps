Problem statement: Nutrition Labels with Screen Readers

For the blind and visually impaired, nutrition labels are not only difficult to access, but also cumbersome to navigate in their current format even when they are able to be located and read through accessibility tools such as screen readers.

Affinity Diagram: Nutrition Labels with Screen Readers

Our affinity diagram gives details about how we want to go about having screen readers be able to process and format nutrition labels in a way that is not just a bunch of information being read. Included are many features that we believe are important as well as accessibility considerations.

Team Personas: Personas that represented users that will benefit from our app

My persona is a man named Keith Cooper who suffers from vision loss due to diabetic retinopathy.

Team Storyboards: Stories of how our users have used our app

My storyboard is about Keith Cooper who is heading over to Publix to pick up a meal for lunch. He cares a lot about his health and needs to know what is in the food he eats. With the app he is to scan the barcode since he struggle to read the tiny print. He can change the speed of the information being read as well as pick out the information he wants to hear.
